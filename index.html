Agent-First RLM Framework

A successful agent-first RLM deployment requires a headless foundation. This framework executes this in three stages:

  • Agentic Grounding (The Commercial Matrix): Here map the business constraints into a deterministic matrix, ensuring the AI has the exact parameters needed to parse intent without hallucination.
  • Intent-Driven Orchestration (Commercial Archetypes): Construct each revenue stream as a headless archetype, shifting from rigid UI click-paths to unbroken API payload passing across the agent layer.
  • Deterministic Execution (Traceable Execution): Execute the architecture using a 12-phase traceability model. This enforces strict integration contracts at every node, guaranteeing zero scope creep and complete backend predictability."

Discovery, Base Reality & Agentic Path

Traditionally, Discovery is about watching business users use a system to find inefficiencies. In Agent-First approach, Discovery acts as Payload and Integration Mapping. This is not observing the user to replicate their clicks; this is observing them to understand exactly actions and what data they need to make a decision, so that exact data can be feed to the agent.

Architecting the Commercial Archetypes

Having mapped the precise business intent and categorized the deficits across Process, System, and Data, the diagnostic phase concludes. We now pivot from business analysis to structural design, translating the identified gaps into a concrete architectural blueprint.

A Note on the Design Phase: Unlike the highly structured templates of the Discovery phases, the Solution Design phase is intentionally fluid. This is a dynamic, collaborative stage where the technical team transitions from analyzing business gaps to actively brainstorming structural solutions. Rather than relying on rigid, pre-filled documents, the artifacts in this phase evolve organically out of architecture design meetings as the team engineers the optimal data and integration blueprints.
